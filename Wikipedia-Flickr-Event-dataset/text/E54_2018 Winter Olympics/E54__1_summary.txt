By Kim BoramPYEONGCHANG, South Korea, Feb. 9 (Yonhap) -- South Korea's skating star Mo Tae-bum represented every athlete in taking the "Olympic Oath" at the opening ceremony of the PyeongChang Winter Olympics Friday, vowing fair competition and sportsmanship.
"In the name of the athletes, we promise to take part in these Olympic Games, respecting and abiding by the rules and in the spirit of fair play.
The Olympic Oath is a solemn promise made by one athlete, judge or official, and one coach at the opening ceremony of the Olympics.
Each oath taker is from the host nation and takes the oath on behalf of all athletes, officials and coaches.
South Korea's speed skater Mo Tae-bum (2nd from L), a coach and an official take an Olympic Oath at the opening Ceremony of the 2018 PyeongChang Winter Olympics in PyeongChang on Feb. 9, 2018.
