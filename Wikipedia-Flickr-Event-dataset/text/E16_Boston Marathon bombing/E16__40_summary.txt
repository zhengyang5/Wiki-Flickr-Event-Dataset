Investigators believe the bombs were hidden in black nylon backpacks and housed inside sealable metal pots called pressure cookers.
Several of the bombs were used in the 2006 attack on trains in Mumbai, India.
The details on the explosives emerged as the chief FBI agent in Boston made a public appeal for any tips to help identify suspects.
A law enforcement official told CBS News that the two bombs that exploded were made to look like discarded property.
The Pakistani Taliban, which claimed responsibility for the 2010 attempt in Times Square, has denied any role in the Boston Marathon attack.
