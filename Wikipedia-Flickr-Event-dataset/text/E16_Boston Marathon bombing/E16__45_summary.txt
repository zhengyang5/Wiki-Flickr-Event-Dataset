AP Glance: Pressure Cooker BombsThe explosives used in the Boston Marathon bombing were crude devices often called "pressure cooker" bombs, according to a person briefed on the investigation.
The discovery includes bags of potassium nitrate, sulfur, headlight bulbs and a container used as a make-shift pressure cooker.
Officials say the attackers remotely detonated a pressure cooker bomb.
One bomb — at a temple in the northern city of Varanasi where five people died — was placed in a pressure cooker and detonated by a timing device.
August 2002: Explosives packed in a pressure cooker shake a shopping mall in Katmandu, the capital of Nepal.
