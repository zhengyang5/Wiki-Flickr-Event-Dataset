For Flight 370 Families, Every Day Is 'Torment'Enlarge this image toggle caption Andy Wong/AP Andy Wong/APFamily members of the passengers aboard Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 have grown increasingly frustrated in the nearly two weeks since the flight disappeared.
For many family members gathered at a Beijing hotel, the glare of media attention can be harsh.
"Every day of this torment that goes by feels like a year to us."
Team member Tseng Yun-chi says they often help relatives with just a pat on the shoulder, a hot cup of tea or a sympathetic ear.
Earlier suggestions that Flight 370 could have been hijacked gave some family members a glimmer of hope.
