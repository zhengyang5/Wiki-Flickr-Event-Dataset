The main shareholder of Malaysia Airlines is looking at the possibility of taking the carrier private to tackle its financial problems following the loss of Flight 370 earlier this year, people with knowledge of the process said Wednesday.
State investment fund Khazanah Nasional Bhd., which owns 69% of Malaysia Airlines' parent company, has said it is studying options to restructure and turn around the airline.
