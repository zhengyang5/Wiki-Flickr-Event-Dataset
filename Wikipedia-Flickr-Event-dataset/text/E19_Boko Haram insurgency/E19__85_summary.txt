DAMASAK, Nigeria (Reuters) - Boko Haram militants have kidnapped more than 400 women and children from the northern Nigerian town of Damasak that was freed this month by troops from Niger and Chad, residents said on Tuesday.
They killed about 50 of them before leaving,” a trader called Souleymane Ali told Reuters in the town.
(Boko Haram) said ‘They are slaves so we’re taking them because they belong to us’,” he said.
A television set is seen in a house that residents say was forcefully seized and occupied by a Boko Haram militant, in Damasak March 24, 2015.
