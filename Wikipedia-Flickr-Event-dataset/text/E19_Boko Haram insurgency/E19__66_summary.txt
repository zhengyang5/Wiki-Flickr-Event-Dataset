Two car bombs exploded at a bustling bus terminal and market in Nigeria's central city of Jos on Tuesday, killing at least 118 people, wounding dozens and leaving streets strewn with bloodied bodies.
There was no immediate claim of responsibility for the twin car bombs.
Dozens of bodies and body parts were covered in grain that had been loaded in the second car bomb, witnesses said.
At least 118 people were killed and dozens wounded, according to Nigeria's National Emergency Management Agency.
Police there detonated a second car bomb Monday.
