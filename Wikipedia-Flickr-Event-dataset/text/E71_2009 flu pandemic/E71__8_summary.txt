Updated Interim Recommendations for the Use of Antiviral Medications in the Treatment and Prevention of Influenza for the 2009-2010 SeasonThe Public Health Emergency determination for 2009 H1N1 Influenza expired on June 23, 2010, terminating Emergency Use Authorizations issued during the pandemic, including some related to antiviral medications.
As of December 4, 2009, influenza A (H1N1) virus (2009 H1N1), is the strain responsible for >99% of influenza in the U.S. during the 2009-2010 influenza season.
ObjectiveTo provide updated recommendations on the use of antiviral agents for treatment and prophylaxis of influenza during the 2009-2010 influenza season.
Influenza illness can present in a range of symptomatology: from a mild upper respiratory infection to an acute, life-threatening illness.
The seasonal influenza vaccine is not expected to provide protection against the 2009 H1N1 influenza or vice-versa.
