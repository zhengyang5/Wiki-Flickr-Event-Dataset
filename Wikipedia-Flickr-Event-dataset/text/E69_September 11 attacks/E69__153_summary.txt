A patriotic rally did not materialize, but neither did a more precipitous drop that might have halted trading under New York Stock Exchange circuit-breaker rules.
Broader markets also fell sharply, with airline and many other transportation issues, retail, entertainment and technology stocks posting dramatic losses.
The Dow's previous record one-day point drop was 617.78 points during the April 14, 2000, meltdown; the percentage drop, however, did not rank among the Dow's 10 biggest falls.
Japan intervened in foreign exchange markets to support the greenback.
"The most important thing today was that the markets opened and function," said Peter McTeague, markets strategist for Greenwich Capital.
