Conceived during the headiest days of the boom, when Iceland's financial viking raiders were marauding through Europe, the project was abruptly halted in late 2008 when the fragile financial edifice its backers had constructed collapsed.
In February ratings agency Fitch restored its investment-grade status, citing its "unorthodox crisis policy response".
As economist Paul Krugman put it: "Iceland broke all the rules, and things are not too bad."
Criminal investigations were launched into the banking elite, and courts found former prime minister Geir Haarde guilty of failing to hold emergency cabinet meetings in run up to financial crisis.
