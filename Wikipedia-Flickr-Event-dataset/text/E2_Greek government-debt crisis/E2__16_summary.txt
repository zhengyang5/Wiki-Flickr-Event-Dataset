During the height of the global financial crisis of 2008, Greek defense expenditures accounted for 3.1 percent of gross domestic product.
Over the past two years, Greek defense spending still amounted to 2.1 percent of G.D.P.
Last August, the €10 billion, or $13 billion, defense budget was trimmed by €516 million.
Other analysts agree that the Greek armed forces can always resist big defense cuts by playing the Turkey card.
Perhaps, analysts said, the Greek armed forces will have to wait for any major restructuring until the country’s economy picks up.
