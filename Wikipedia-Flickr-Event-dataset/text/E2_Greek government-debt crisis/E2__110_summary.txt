Tax evasion in Greece is estimated to be between 11billion euros and 16 billion euros annually, a study has found.
In an public opinion poll conducted by DiaNEOsis in 2016, 85,5% of respondents stated that they consider tax evasion to be equivalent to theft.
According to several studies, tax evasion in Greece country is substantially higher than in other developed countries.
According to the studies:Revenue lost due to personal income tax evasion ranges from 1.9% to 4.7% of annual GDP.
For legal entities, revenue lost from tax evasion and tax avoidance is estimated at around 0.15% of GDP.
