Photo: Associated PressATHENS—Of all the challenges Greece has faced in recent years, prodding its citizens to pay their taxes has been one of the most difficult.
Forcing them to pay more in taxes would put even more out of business—and more Greeks out of work.
“The Greek economy would collapse if the government were to force these people to pay taxes,” one senior government official said.
Many companies report they pay their workers the minimum wage, then supplement it under the table to avoid having to pay higher payroll taxes.
