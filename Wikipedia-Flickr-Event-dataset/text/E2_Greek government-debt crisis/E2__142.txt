The European Central Bank delivered some holiday cheer last month to beleaguered crisis-fighting policymakers; the ECB’s move to provide long-term loans to euro-zone banks helped drive down yields on government debt, helping assure officials that their Christmas vacations wouldn’t be interrupted by another spasm of the crisis.

But largely unnoticed was a fly in the eggnog: data from Eurostat showing limited competitiveness gains by countries in the euro-zone periphery relative to Germany and other countries in the currency bloc’s core.
