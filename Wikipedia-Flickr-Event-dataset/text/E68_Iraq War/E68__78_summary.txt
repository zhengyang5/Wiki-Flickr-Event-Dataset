Pentagon: Saddam is POWDeposed Iraqi leader's status could affect eventual trialWASHINGTON (CNN) -- The Pentagon has declared former Iraqi President Saddam Hussein an enemy prisoner of war, officials have said.
That would interfere with U.S. plans to turn the former Iraqi leader over to an Iraqi war crimes tribunal.
The Pentagon continued to avoid publicly calling Saddam a prisoner of war until early Friday evening.
Red Cross seeks access to SaddamMeanwhile, the International Committee of the Red Cross is negotiating to visit Saddam, an official with the organization told CNN on Friday.
It is not known if Saddam will accept the visit, officials have told CNN.
