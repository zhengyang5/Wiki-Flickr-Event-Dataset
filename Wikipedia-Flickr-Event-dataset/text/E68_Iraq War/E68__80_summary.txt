The occupation of Iraq continues to get worse for George Bush and Tony Blair.
The occupiers who invaded Iraq in the name (partly) of bringing democracy are being accused of flouting democracy themselves.
While the Hutton inquiry looms as the main Iraq worry for the prime minister, the primary problem for Bush is the chaos in Iraq.
Worst of all for Washington, Sistani has made it clear that no government which is undemocratically appointed will have the right to ask American troops to stay.
Washington is trying to argue that if there are to be direct elections, the transfer of power will have to be delayed.
