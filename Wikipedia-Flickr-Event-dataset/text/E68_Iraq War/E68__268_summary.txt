The US and British occupation of Iraq has accelerated recruitment to the ranks of Osama bin Laden's terrorist network and made the world a less safe place, according to a leading London-based think-tank.
The IISS report, published yesterday, says that the Iraq invasion"galvanised" al-Qa'ida while weakening the campaign against terrorism.
The report amounts to a sustained condemnation of US and British tactics, especially during the post-war period.
The report adds that later swoops, which led to mass arrests, and aggressive house searches "perversely inspired insurgent violence".
Jonathan Stevenson, the editor of the survey, said: "Invading Iraq damaged the war on terror, there is no doubt about that.
