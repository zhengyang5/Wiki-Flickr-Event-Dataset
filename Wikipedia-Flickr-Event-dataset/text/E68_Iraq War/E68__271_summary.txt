In 'Suicide Bombers in Iraq,' Mohammed Hafez seeks to understand what drives such men and, in rare cases, women.
Analysts worldwide have been unable to arrive at a useful socioeconomic or psychological profile of suicide bombers in Iraq.
The only consensus among analysts, Hafez says, is that suicide bombers are not simply crazy or born violent.
The bad news is that the United States faces greater challenges in Iraq than suicide terrorism, insidious as it is.
But after reading 'Suicide Bombers in Iraq' and 'A Poisonous Affair,' it's hard not to see it as more than that.
