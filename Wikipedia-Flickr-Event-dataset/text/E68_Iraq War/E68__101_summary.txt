Thirty-eight percent in Anbar province, a focal point of the surge, now rate local security positively; none did so six months ago.
In one measure, the number of Iraqis who rate their local security positively (43 percent) is no better than it was in March.
At best, only 18 percent of Iraqis say it has improved security in surge areas; at worst, just six percent say it's improved the pace of economic development.
