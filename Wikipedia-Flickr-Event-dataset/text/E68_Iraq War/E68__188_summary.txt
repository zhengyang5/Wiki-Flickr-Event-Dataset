Mr. Sadr’s surprise decision in August to support Mr. Maliki, his longtime antagonist, for a second term as prime minister effectively decided the election in Mr. Maliki’s favor.
Mr. Sadr’s followers exacted repayment in critical government posts.
“His presence in Iraq, it will strengthen the resolve of our Sadrist brothers to serve the people of Iraq,” said Muhammad al-Khafaji, a former bodyguard of Mr. Sadr’s who won a seat in the election.
Mr. Sadr’s return from Qum, a seat of Shiite scholarship in Iran, had long been rumored.
One analyst suggested that the success of an emerging Sadrist leadership inside Iraq might have motivated Mr. Sadr to come back.
