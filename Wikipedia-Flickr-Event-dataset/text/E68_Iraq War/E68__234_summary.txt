Lack of clean drinking water and poor sanitation has led to 5,000 people in northern Iraq contracting cholera.
"The disease is spreading very fast," Dr Juan Abdallah, a senior official in Kurdistan's health ministry, told a UN agency.
The number of Iraqi refugees stands at 4.2 million of whom two million have been displaced within Iraq.
"In Sulaimaniyah and Kirkuk, at least 42 per cent of the population don't have access to clean water and proper sewage systems."
Unicef says that local reports suggest that only 30 per cent of people in Sulaimaniyah have clean drinking water.
