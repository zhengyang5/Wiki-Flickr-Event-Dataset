By the next morning, it was clear that “I can’t breathe” were the words that would be most remembered from this troubled year.
Since the grand jury’s decision, “I can’t breathe” has become a peculiarly powerful rallying cry.
As an expression of solidarity, “I can’t breathe” is easily modified into the more inclusive “We can’t breathe.” Now it is not just about empathizing with Garner’s dying moment but about mobilizing that moment into social action.
At the New York rally, signs like “Justice can’t breathe” and “Our democracy can’t breathe” extended the metaphor of breath well beyond the physical realm.
