KAMA ADO, Afghanistan- Children's shoes, bits of charred carpet and cooking pots litter what is left of this hamlet, along with dead cows and sheep.
On Monday provincial officials brought reporters to see what they said was the destruction done by U.S. bombs at Kama Ado, about a half-hour walk along a trail from the nearest town.
Witnesses and survivors say U.S. warplanes dropped more than 25 bombs in four passes over the village on Saturday.
