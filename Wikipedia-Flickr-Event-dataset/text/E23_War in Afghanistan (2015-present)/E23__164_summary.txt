The Central Intelligence Agency, while increasing the frequency of drone strikes in Pakistan, has reduced civilian casualties, a U.S. official and independent analysts said.
The U.S. doesn’t publicly acknowledge the drone attacks against targets in Pakistan, although the strikes are perhaps the best-known CIA covert activity.
Husain Haqqani, Pakistan’s ambassador to the U.S., indirectly commented on the issue in a Jan. 11 interview when asked about drone strikes.
‘Better Surveillance’The CIA has been working to avoid the civilian deaths, said Roggio of The Long War Journal.
Last year, Philip Alston, then the United Nations’ special rapporteur on extra-judicial executions, called for a halt in CIA strikes.
