David Cameron has increased the number of troops serving in Afghanistan for the first time since the UK’s withdrawal began.
Obama delays US troop withdrawal from ‘precarious’ Afghanistan Read moreThe UK withdrew all combat troops from Afghanistan in 2014 after 13 years of fighting the Taliban, but a support mission of 450 stayed on to train domestic forces.
Cameron fought the 2010 election promising total withdrawal of UK troops from Afghanistan after a decade-long war started in the aftermath of the 9/11 terror attacks in the US.
His spokesman on Friday denied the UK was getting drawn back into the conflict, which has cost more than 450 British lives.
Britain is not the only country that has extended the stay of its troops in Afghanistan.
