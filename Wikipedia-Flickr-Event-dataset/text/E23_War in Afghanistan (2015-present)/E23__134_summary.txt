“WELCOME to Freedom's Frontier,” reads a wooden sign at the pine-clad headquarters of America's Manas airbase in Kyrgyzstan.
With its picnic tables, mountain views and community-outreach programmes, this site provides a tranquil vantage-point for the war in Afghanistan, just 90 minutes' flight away.
It has long bristled at America's military presence in Central Asia and has just offered the Kyrgyzstani government $2.3 billion in aid.
Since Kyrgyzstan faces energy shortages and a mass return of migrant labourers from Russia, it may be susceptible to persuasion.
It is stitching together a “northern distribution network” to bring non-lethal supplies such as water and fuel into Afghanistan through Russia and Central Asia.
