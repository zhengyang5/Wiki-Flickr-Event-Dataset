Afghan troops in the besieged town of Sangin were fighting to the death yesterday after a relief mission failed to break through Taliban lines as the extremists threatened to overrun the town.
The fall of Sangin would be humiliating for the Afghan government and symbolic for British and American forces which suffered heavy losses in the battle to secure the district before the end of combat operations there last year.
Hellish scenes were reported inside police headquarters, where about 200 soldiers and police were holding out against the insurgents after two days of fighting.
The dead and wounded were strewn around the building, which is under constant mortar and rocket attack by Taliban fighters.
