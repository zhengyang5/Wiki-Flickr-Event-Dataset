KABUL (Reuters) - Taliban fighters on motorbikes have carried out hit-and-run attacks on Afghan forces trying to clear Kunduz city of insurgents, more than a week after the militant movement briefly seized the provincial capital.
Afghan security forces sit on top of a vehicle as they patrol outside of Kunduz city, October 1, 2015.
Afghan security forces prepare to check on reports of a possible ambush by the Taliban on the Baghlan-Kunduz highway, Afghanistan October 1, 2015.
REUTERS/StringerBattles have raged around Kunduz for the last nine days as government forces, backed by U.S. air strikes, have tried to drive out Taliban fighters.
Security forces reported significant progress in trying to retake Kunduz on Monday, and some shops in the centre opened for the first time since it fell to Taliban fighters.
