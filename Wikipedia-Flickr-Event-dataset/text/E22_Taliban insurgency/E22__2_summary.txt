The hostility between India and Pakistan, ongoing for more than 60 years, lies at the heart of the current war in Afghanistan.
Today the Tajiks, who constitute 27% of the Afghan population, make up 70% of the officers in the Afghan army.
Beyond this indigenous conflict looms the much more dangerous hostility between the two nuclear-armed regional powers, India and Pakistan.
The simple truth is that the Taliban are doing as well as they are in Afghanistan because they are being supported by Pakistan.
