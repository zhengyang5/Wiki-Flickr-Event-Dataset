Armed police step up patrols at St Pancras in wake of Charlie Hebdo Paris massacreArmed police have stepped up patrols at the station PA Wire/Press Association ImagesBritish Transport Police (BTP) said they have increased armed patrols at St Pancras station in King’s Cross in the wake of a massacre in Paris.
A spokesman said: “Since 2012, we have deployed armed officers within London.
“In light of the events in Paris, we have increased patrols at St Pancras, in order to maintain and provide enhanced visibility and reassurance to the public.
At 10.30am, 24 hours after the shootings at satirical magazine Charlie Hebdo, officers and police staff paused “in solidarity and sympathy”.
British Transport Police chief constable Paul Crowther said his teams had spoken with their French counterparts at the St Pancras Eurostar terminal and offered their condolences.
