Martin Ferguson announces he will retire from parliament at next electionUpdatedA member of "Labor Party royalty", former frontbencher Martin Ferguson, has announced he will quit politics at the September election.
Mr Ferguson, who is the member for the Victorian seat of Batman, says it was a "personal decision".
In Opposition Mr Ferguson served in various portfolios including employment, immigration, transport and primary industries.
Long history in labour movementBefore entering parliament, Mr Ferguson rose through the ranks of the Miscellaneous Workers' Union until finally serving as president of the ACTU for six years.
"My main motivation has been to get Australians into decent, well-paying jobs," Mr Ferguson said today of his career.
