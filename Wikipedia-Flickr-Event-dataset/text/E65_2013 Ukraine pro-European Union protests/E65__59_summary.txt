Twenty years after the collapse of the Soviet Union, Ukraine is slipping back under Kremlin control.
It is the first major defeat for the EU in its eastward march since the fall of Communism.
Ukraine’s bizarre predicament was captured by Moody’s when it cut the country’s debt rating to C grade in September because of the forthcoming EU deal.
Germany has demanded her release as condition for any EU deal, but she is still viewed as a major political threat by President Viktor Yanukovych.
Yet for all the fury with Ukraine in Brussels, there is no disguising the damage done to EU prestige and power.
