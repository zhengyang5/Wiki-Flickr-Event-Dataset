NEW DELHI: Stepping up its relief and rescue operations in earthquake-hit Nepal, the Indian Air Force on Monday sent 12 aircraft carrying relief material.
The IAF’s Mi-17 helicopters made six sorties and evacuated at least 87 casualties and dropped 3.5 tons of relief load.
“The aid that is being sent to Nepal is what was asked for by the Nepal army.
A satellite communication link has been established between the Nepal army headquarters and India’s Army Engineer Task Force.
On Monday, the IAF sent five tonnes of milk, light vehicles, a reverse osmosis plant, oxygen regenerators, medicines, blankets, tents, 14 tonnes of food to Nepal.
