A Brisbane software development company is using its hacking powers for good, mobilising staff and members of the public to help map earthquake-hit areas of Nepal.
Managing director of NetEngine Bruce Stronge said anyone with a basic knowledge of computing and interest in helping the people of Nepal could join their digital crowd-mapping army.
NetEngine is helping humanitarian organisations on the ground in Nepal by facilitating crowd-mapping of remote regions.
Photo: Supplied"Every time there's a humanitarian disaster in a third world country, agencies like Red Cross have very little information on how to get to the people who are in need," he said.
"Google tends to have detailed maps in places where there are commercial interests, and that doesn't tend to happen in places like the border of Mali and West Africa were things like ebola break out."
