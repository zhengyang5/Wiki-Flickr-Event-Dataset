Thousands of villages devastated and up to 90% of clinics and schools in some districts unusable, as Kathmandu appealing for help from international donorsThe death toll from the earthquake that struck Nepal on Saturday has passed 6,000, and many thousands are still unaccounted for.
The latest police report has confirmed 6,260 fatalities, with about 14,000 injured and thousands still unaccounted for.
Thousands of villages have been devastated, with up to 90% of clinics and schools in some districts rendered unusable.
Large numbers have left Kathmandu on free buses provided by the government, often to check on relatives in other regions.
