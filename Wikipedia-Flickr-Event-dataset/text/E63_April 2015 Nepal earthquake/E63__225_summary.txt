John Kerry offers condolences to those affected as state department says it is checking on US embassy personnel and citizensNepal earthquake: US to send disaster response team and $1m in aidNepal quake: more than 1,000 people dead after tremor near Kathmandu Read moreThe US is sending a disaster response team and $1m in aid to Nepal, following a devastating earthquake that shook three countries.
The White House and Secretary of State John Kerry offered condolences along with pledging the support.
The magnitude 7.8 earthquake outside the capital Kathmandu killed more than 1,000 people in Nepal, India and Bangladesh.
Kerry said in a statement that the US stood with the people of Nepal and sent heartfelt sympathies.
He said USAID was preparing to deploy the disaster assistance response team and was activating an urban search and rescue team.
