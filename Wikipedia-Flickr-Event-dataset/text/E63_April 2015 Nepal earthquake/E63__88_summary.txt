Excited reports about the Indian Army’s rescue operation has led to the accusation that Indian television crews were were covering the disaster like a TV serial.
more-inThe first signs of a backlash in Nepal against the Indian media, manifested in the furiously trending hashtag #GoHomeIndianMedia, were evident from early last week.
At the military airfield near the Tribhuvan international airport, arguments between Indian journalists and Nepal’s liaison officer, who was coordinating media personnel, was routine all through last week.
What was created therefore, was a situation in which TV journalists inadvertently became embedded reporters.
As more and more access was granted by the Indian Army, there followed a series of excited reports, about how the Indian Army was rescuing and giving help to thousands of people in remote areas.
