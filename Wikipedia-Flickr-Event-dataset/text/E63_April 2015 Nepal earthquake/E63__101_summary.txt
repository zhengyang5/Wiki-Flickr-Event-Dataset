Alongside the relief operation in earthquake-ravaged Kathmandu is another smaller, but nonetheless important effort: protecting Nepal’s most precious temples and monuments from further damage.
Before the quake, the tiny mountainous country’s economy relied heavily on tourism rupees that these culturally significant sites generated, along with Mount Everest.
“This is our heritage,” Ranjitkar said.
They rely on a crowdmapping tool that people use to submit photos, descriptions and locations of damaged sites.
