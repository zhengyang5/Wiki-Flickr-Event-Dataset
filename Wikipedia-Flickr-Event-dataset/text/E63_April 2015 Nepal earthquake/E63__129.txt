BRUSSELS, APR 30 - EU Commissioner for Humanitarian Aid and Crisis Management, Christos Stylianides , is arriving in Nepal on Thursday, following the devastating earthquake that hit the country on Saturday. The EU Commissioner will be accompanied by United Nations Under-Secretary-General Valerie Amos, who responsible for the international coordination of the humanitarian response.

During his visit here, the EU crisis management chief will join humanitarian aid and civil protection experts from the European Union who are deployed to Nepal to assess the needs and coordinate with international partners.

The visit comes after urgent mobilisation of funding (& eu ro;3 million) from the European Commission and first response teams and assets from the Member States. So far, 16 countries have provided support to Nepal with EU coordination, via the EU Civil Protection Mechanism.

EU funding is helping meet the most urgent needs in the worst affected areas of Nepal: clean water, medicines, shelter and telecommunications. Belgium, the Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Poland, Sweden, Spain and the United Kingdom, as well as Norway, are participating in this joint effort so far.
