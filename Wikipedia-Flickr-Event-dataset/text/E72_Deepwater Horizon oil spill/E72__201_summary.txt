The oil slick spreading across the Gulf of Mexico is pushing Canada's energy regulator to take a harder look at a bid by several major oil companies to relax safety regulations for offshore drilling in the Arctic.
Similar questions are being asked across Canada and the U.S., where President Barack Obama recently moved to give industry access to new ground for offshore drilling.
The rule stipulates that any company drilling an offshore well must also be able to drill a relief well in the same season.
The ability to drill a relief well in the same season is especially important in the Arctic, where thickening ice typically forces a halt to all drilling by December.
