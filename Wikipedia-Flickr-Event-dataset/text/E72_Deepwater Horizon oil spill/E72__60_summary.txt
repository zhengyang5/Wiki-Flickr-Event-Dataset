It was always inconceivable that President Obama would consider using a nuclear explosion to cut the flow of oil from the unrelenting seabed gusher in the Gulf of Mexico — no matter how many other options faltered.
Now it’s simply official, as reported by my colleague Bill Broad: “Nuclear Option on Gulf Oil Spill?
No Way, U.S. Says.”It’s one thing to point to four successful instances in which nuclear explosions were used in the Soviet Union in decades past to seal gas wells after blowouts.
