Oslo: The forensic examination of the Deepwater Horizon blowout preventer (BOP) is now concluded, and DNV has submitted its report to the U.S. Bureau of Ocean Energy Management, Regulation and Enforcement (BOEMRE), who today made the report publically available.
DNV has utilized its forensic investigation expertise from the Columbus, Ohio office and its subsea equipment (BOP) expertise from the Houston, Texas office in the project.
DNV is engaged in verification, classification and asset risk management offshore in the Gulf of Mexico and within risk management of onshore pipelines and refining.
This allows DNV Columbus to provide our clients with the highest quality engineering solutions to materials-related problems, in addition to tempering our research with practical field experience.
This diverse nature of the staff allows DNV Columbus to address a broad range of materials-related problems.
