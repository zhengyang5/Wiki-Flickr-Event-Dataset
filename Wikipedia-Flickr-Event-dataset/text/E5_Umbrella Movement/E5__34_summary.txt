The Umbrella Movement has become Hong Kong's Summer of Love, but with politics instead of drugs.
For the last two months we've seen the same tumult and transformation take hold in Hong Kong.
This is a group who see themselves as Hong Kongers first and Chinese second, and a generation in which suspicion of elites in both Beijing and Hong Kong runs deep.
Looked at with historical hindsight, the Umbrella Movement will prove to be a similar turning point, one seared into the city's memory by the birth pangs of a new generation.
