Welcome to a distinctively 21st century protest movement with its feet and approach firmly in the ‘now’ but with roots and inspiration in previous times and places.
While this is a uniquely ‘Chinese’ uprising, many of its implications and lessons are truly global.
The Umbrella Movement in Hong Kong did not emerge overnight; it has been brewing for many years, certainly since British colonialism formally withdrew in July 1997.
Local opposition is growing, especially from business interests which argue that the ‘business’ of Hong Kong must not be interrupted.
Events in Hong Kong have again demonstrated the growing importance of social media and the power and immediacy of communication.
