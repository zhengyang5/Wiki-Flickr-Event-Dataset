Associated Press - Tsunami survivors cling tightly to faith across ravaged regionBy Brian MurphyCopyright 2005 The Associated PressBANDA ACEH, Indonesia - When the midday call to prayers sounded, the boys put down their brooms and entered the mosque.
And nearly all the religious responses to the tsunami eventually flirt with a bottomless riddle: How could a gentle God let this happen?
But simple acts across the region showed a remarkable sense of community in places where clashes pit faith against faith.
In the fishing hamlets of southern India, villagers made offerings to appease "mother" sea by offering milk and burning pungent camphor.
"Either there is no God, or God must be cruel to do this."
