Martin PlautBBC Africa editorThe tsunami crashed through many fishermen's homesWhile the tsunami of 26 December 2004 did its worst damage in South-East Asia, the giant waves also travelled across the Indian Ocean to East Africa.
Somalia, already one of the poorest and most war-torn countries of the region, was worst affected, with 289 people reported dead or missing.
Livelihoods lostWhen the tsunami struck it swept away boats, inundated plots and destroyed around 800 buildings.
New townA new town is now under construction in Hafun - this time built 500 metres from the sea.
Aid agencies have helped the fishing fleet get back in the water, with Action Aid so far providing around 40 boats.
