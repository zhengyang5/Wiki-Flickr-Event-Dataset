Chris Patten, the last British governor of Hong Kong, is chancellor of the University of Oxford.
It is not wholly accurate to say that the eyes of the entire world are on Hong Kong.
Hong Kong is a great international center, whose freedoms and autonomy were guaranteed in a treaty registered at the United Nations.
How Hong Kong develops its democracy is completely within the sphere of the autonomy of Hong Kong.
The British Parliament summarized what had been said and promised in a report on Hong Kong in 2000: “The Chinese government has therefore formally accepted that it is for the Hong Kong government to determine the extent and nature of democracy in Hong Kong.”So, what next?
