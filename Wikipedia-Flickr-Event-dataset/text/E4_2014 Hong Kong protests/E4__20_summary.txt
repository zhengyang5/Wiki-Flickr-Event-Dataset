This is the most serious political crisis in Hong Kong since its reversion to Chinese rule in 1997.
By Monday evening, September 29, massive crowds totalling around 180,000 had gathered in three locations around Hong Kong.
“This is already much bigger than anything the Beijing or Hong Kong authorities expected,” commented Larry Diamond of Stanford University in the New York Times.
The August NPC ruling is just one element in a plan that aims to cage the Hong Kong democracy struggle.
Hong Kong now constitutes a major dilemma for Xi and his attempts to consolidate his control within the regime.
