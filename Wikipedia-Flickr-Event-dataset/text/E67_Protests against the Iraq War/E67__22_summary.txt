Antiwar rallies across the worldLONDON, England -- Antiwar demonstrations are taking place across the world Saturday, as the chances of a diplomatic solution to the Iraq crisis appear to fade.
Tens of thousands of Yemenis heeded President Ali Abdullah Saleh's call to turn out for anti-war rallies that were held amid tight security measures.
Some 3,000 Greek Cypriots marched to the U.S. Embassy and hung cloth banners with anti-war messages on the barbed-wire barricades.
In Melbourne, antiwar protesters held vigils, picnics, education sessions and marches.
Polls show almost 90 percent of Russians oppose a war in Iraq, but there have been few anti-war demonstrations.
