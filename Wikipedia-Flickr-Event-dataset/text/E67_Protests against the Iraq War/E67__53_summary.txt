The vets turned both sides of Constitution into a bitter, charged gantlet for the war protesters.
Some broke into "The Star-Spangled Banner" as war protesters sought to hand out pamphlets.
Others crowned the Lone Sailor statue at the Navy Memorial on Pennsylvania Avenue with a pink tiara that had "Women for Peace" written across it.
"This is sacred ground to us," said Rick De Marco, 62, a Vietnam veteran from Cleveland.
By 2 p.m., with the war protesters across the Potomac River, the metal detectors had come down.
