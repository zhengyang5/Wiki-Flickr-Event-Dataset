It was the second deadliest conflict in the world last year, but it hardly registered in the international headlines.
As Syria, Iraq and Afghanistan dominated the news agenda, Mexico’s drug wars claimed 23,000 lives during 2016 — second only to Syria, where 50,000 people died as a result of the civil war.
In comparison, there were 17,000 conflict deaths in Mexico in 2015 and 15,000 in 2014 according to the IISS.
World conflict deaths fallThe number of conflict fatalities globally edged down last year, from 167,000 to 157,000, according to the IISS.
But there were 1,000 more deaths in Afghanistan last year than 2015 and 4,000 more in Iraq.
