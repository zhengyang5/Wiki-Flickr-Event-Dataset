Mexico's Drug War: A Rigged Fight?
Amid a fight by drug cartels, hundreds of families are fleeing the cotton-farming towns of the valley, a stretch of border 50 miles east of Ciudad Juarez.
Mexican drug cartels are terrorizing residents, emptying the area.
Mexico's drug cartels are the major foreign supplier of marijuana and methamphetamines to the United States, and Mexico is a main conduit for cocaine coming mainly from Colombia.
