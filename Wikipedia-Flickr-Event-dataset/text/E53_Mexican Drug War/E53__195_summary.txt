When policy makers in Washington labeled Mexico a “failing” state, the Mexican authorities were rightly annoyed by the claim.
Indeed, outside the military, the Guatemalan state is a feeble entity by almost any indicator.
As a point of comparison, the murder rate in the United States is currently 5.9 per 100,000 people.
The weakness of the state, the pervasive violence, the widespread corruption, and the country’s strategic location for drug trafficking are creating a very dangerous cocktail.
The approximately $10-20 million that have been allotted to Guatemala under the Merida Initiative to combat organized crime are grossly insufficient to have any visible impact on the problem.
