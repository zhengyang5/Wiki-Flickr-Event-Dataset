Tactical voting could save Jim Murphy’s East Renfrewshire seat but elsewhere it is “doomed to fail”EFFORTS by unionist politicians to persuade Scots to vote tactically in order to stop the SNP are doomed to fail, according to an analysis by the Electoral Reform Society.
It has identified 10 constituencies, including Scottish Labour leader Jim Murphy’s East Renfrewshire seat, where tactical voting could hit the SNP because the non-SNP combined vote is significantly higher than the predicted nationalist vote.
But Willie Sullivan, the society’s Scottish director, cautioned that while certain incumbents might benefit from such tactical voting, “in reality voters find it quite difficult to vote for parties they would not normally support”.
