A survey by Bloomberg predicted the pound could tumble as much as 10 per cent in the event of a Yes vote.
Ongoing uncertainty over what currency an independent Scotland might use in the event of a Yes vote has plagued the referendum, and unsettled major financial institutions.
UBS predicted Scotland’s GDP would nosedive by between 4 and 5 per cent after a Yes vote as the country’s banking sector relocated to England.
It said Scottish GDP fell 6.7 per cent in 2009 after the collapse of Royal Bank of Scotland and Bank of Scotland.
Global financiers UBS are telling the world that if Scotland votes Yes we will be plunged into an economic mess."
