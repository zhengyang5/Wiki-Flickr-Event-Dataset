The lead held by the “No” campaign to reject independence has slumped to 6 points from 22 points at the start of August.
The currency is centre-stage in the heated Scottish referendum debate.
“The currency market is where it will be most acutely felt,” said George Godber, manager of the CF Miton UK Value Opportunities Fund.
In the currency market, though, the cost of hedging against near term uncertainty jumped.
But traders also cited demand for sterling puts - or bets the currency will fall - as the Scottish referendum nears.
