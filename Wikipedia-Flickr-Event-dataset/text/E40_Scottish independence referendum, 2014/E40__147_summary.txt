Multrees Investor Services, a manager of bank accounts for the wealth management industry, said it alone had moved hundreds of millions of pounds on behalf of several wealth managers.
“The big question is, ‘Is my cash safe in a Scottish bank?’,” he said.
The decision to suspend hostilities reflects mounting concerns in Whitehall and the City about the implications of a Yes vote.
Wealth managers report clients moving deposits out of Scottish banks, and pension funds out of the stock market and into cash.
Big Scottish financial institutions, including Royal Bank of Scotland and Lloyds Banking Group, have said they are concerned about the consequences of a Yes vote and are working on contingency plans.
