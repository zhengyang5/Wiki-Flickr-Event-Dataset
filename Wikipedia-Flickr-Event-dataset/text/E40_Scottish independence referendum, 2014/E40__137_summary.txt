In an earlier white paper on independence in 2009, the SNP described a sterling currency union as a first step towards membership of the euro.
Sturgeon hit back at the criticisms as she warned that an independent Scotland may refuse to accept its share of UK liabilities if the rest of the UK rejects a currency union.
The deputy first minister told Good Morning Scotland on BBC Radio Scotland: "Sterling is a shared asset.
Sturgeon's remarks came after the pro-UK campaign intensified its warnings of the dangers of a currency union.
She told Today the SNP had no alternative to a currency union with the UK.
