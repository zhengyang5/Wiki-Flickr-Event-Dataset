There is no alternative base for the UK's nuclear deterrent than its existing sites in Scotland, leaving it with the prospect of having nowhere to go should Scots vote for independence.
They are only now beginning to face the consequences of a possible future Scottish parliament voting to get rid of the Trident nuclear weapons bases at Coulport and Faslane.
The study, by John Ainslie of the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament, concludes that there are no viable alternatives.
Philip Hammond, the defence secretary, has suggested that Scotland would be forced to pay towards the costs of relocating Trident.
