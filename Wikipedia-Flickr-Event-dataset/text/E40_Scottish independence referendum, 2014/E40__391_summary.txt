Police Scotland say they have completed their investigation and "no criminality has been uncovered"POLICE Scotland have completed their investigation into comments made by Scottish Conservative leader Ruth Davidson and have said they have found "no criminality".
The investigation was triggered by comments made by Davidson just after polls closed in the referendum campaign that No campaigners had been "taking tallies" of postal vote samples around Scotland.
We have been in regular contact with the Crown Office and Procurator Fiscal Service and they are aware of the findings of the enquiry."
In September 2015 the Crown office considered whether to pursue further action against Davidson.
The findings have prompted the Crown office that for them the issue is "now closed".
