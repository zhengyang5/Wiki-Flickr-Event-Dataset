The concept itself involves the acceptance of a whole new way of thinking about social security policy in terms of the functions it can, should and does perform.
However, the tendency is to view a CBI within the confines of rather narrow and limiting debates on the future of social security policy.
However, questions remain as to how effective the labour market is, and will continue to be, in providing sustainable and meaningful employment opportunities for all.
Thus, any anti-poverty strategy that has as a primary focus the promotion of labour market participation may only be addressing part of the problem.
Considering the relationship between social and economic policy in the new Scotland, those questions should and could come to the forefront of debate.
