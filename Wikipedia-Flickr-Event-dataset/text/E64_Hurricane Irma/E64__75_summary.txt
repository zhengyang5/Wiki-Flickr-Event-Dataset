Two months after Hurricanes Irma and Maria charged through the Caribbean, donors this week pledged hundreds of millions of dollars to rebuild.
The UN estimates the cost of recovery at more than $5 billion in Dominica, Barbuda, and the British Virgin Islands alone.
After a series of hurricanes in the 1990s and without access to concessional financing, the country took on commercial debt.
Gail Hurley, a UN policy specialist on development financing, was wary of efforts to “tinker around the edges with the rules”, saying that new mechanisms are needed.
Other groups have called for a moratorium on debt payments, further forgiveness of loans, and new climate change debt swap financing .
