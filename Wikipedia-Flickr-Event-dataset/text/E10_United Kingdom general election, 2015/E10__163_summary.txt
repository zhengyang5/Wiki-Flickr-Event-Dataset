Conservatives fined £70,000 over election campaign expensesSimon Day, the Party Treasurer, reported to the PoliceParty made 'numerous failures' in reporting expenses during 2015 General ElectionTories accused of 'unreasonable uncooperative conduct'A dozen police forces send files to the CPSThe Conservative Party has been fined £70,000 following an investigation into election campaign expenses, the Electoral Commission has just announced.
The Party failed to declare a total of £275,813 in campaign spending, an investigation has found.
The Commission has also reported Simon Day, the Party Treasurer, to the Metropolitan Police over the spending allegations.
