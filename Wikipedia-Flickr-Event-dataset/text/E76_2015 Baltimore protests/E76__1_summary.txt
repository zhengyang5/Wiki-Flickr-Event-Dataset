Hundreds of people raised their hands and turned their backs on officers outside the Baltimore Police Department's Western District station Saturday evening, part of a protest over injuries a man suffered while being arrested earlier in the week.
At the Western District, residents were upset about injuries to Freddie Gray, 27, who remains in a coma after being injured during an arrest.
According to a police timeline, he was fine when he was loaded into a van to be taken to the district station, but was injured by the time he got out.
