Sick with sadness and rage, I sank down in front of the TV and touched the face on the screen.
I had no idea who had killed him, but I immediately knew why: The stakes in Kashmir had simply become too high to tolerate an honest, moderate voice.
Sometimes I saw the mutilated bodies of young Kashmiri guerrilla suspects dumped by roadsides, sometimes the charred bodies of young Indian soldiers torn to shreds by grenade blasts.
Many Indians blamed Pakistan, suggesting that its intelligence agencies had decided to eliminate a persuasive, anti-Pakistan voice.
Some Pakistani guerrilla groups blamed India, suggesting that its agents had used Lone to tar them as terrorists.
