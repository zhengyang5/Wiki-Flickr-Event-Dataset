In a landmark judgment, the J&K High Court has observed that the Article 370 is a “permanent” provision of the Constitution and that it cannot be “abrogated, repealed or even amended”.
“(The) Article 370, notwithstanding its title ‘temporary provision’ is a permanent provision of the Constitution.
It cannot be abrogated, repealed or even amended as mechanism provided under Clause (3) of Article 370 is no more available,” the court observed in its judgment on a case challenging the reservation benefit in promotions to the employees.
The court observed that Article 370 embodied “conceptual framework of relationship” between the Union of India and Jammu and Kashmir.
The limited sovereignty or special status stands guaranteed under Article 370 of the Constitution,” it added.
