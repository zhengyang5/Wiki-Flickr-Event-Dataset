Lush and awesomely beautiful; prosperous and strategically important; a Muslim state with special privileges within the union of India - all of this is Kashmir.
As a princely state, Kashmir was entitled at the time of partition to choose whether it would join India or Pakistan.
The rebels set up their own ''azad'' (free) Kashmir government and sought assistance from the government of Pakistan to ''liberate'' the rest of Kashmir.
For above and beyond its geopolitical importance, Kashmir is a mirror for Muslim aspirations within a Hindu nation.
''If Kashmir would opt to go to Pakistan,'' a Muslim sage said, ''we in India would be in danger of becoming a Hindu (nation).
