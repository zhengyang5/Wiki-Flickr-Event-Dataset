The All Jammu and Kashmir Muslim Conference was founded in Srinagar in October 1932 and Abdullah was elected its President.
From the All India Kashmir Committee in Lahore came an angry poster addressed by the poet Iqbal to the ‘dumb Muslims of Kashmir’.
The leaders of the Kashmir Muslim Conference shifted to Muzaffarabad in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir, leaving Sheikh Abdullah in control of the valley itself.
The Indian Army moved troops to the area from Srinagar and artillery duels became a daily nightmare for the locals.
There are now three large states in the region: India, Pakistan and Bangladesh, with a combined population of well over a billion people.
