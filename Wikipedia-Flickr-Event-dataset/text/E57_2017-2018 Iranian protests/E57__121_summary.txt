The impacts of climate change are among the environmental challenges facing Iran that helped spark protests in dozens of cities across the Islamic republic.
"You have climate change, shortage of water, they can't grow their crops, and now they're getting their cash handouts taken away," said Handjani.
She said the role of climate change on the protests is "massive" and underreported by the media.
In recent years, there's been a recognition at the highest levels of the Iranian government that climate change poses an acute threat.
Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has called on the country to address climate change.
