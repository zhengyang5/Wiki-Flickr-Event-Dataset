Atop the South Carolina State House, the U.S. flag and South Carolina’s palmetto flag flew at half-staff as the manhunt for Roof ended with his capture in North Carolina and prayer vigils were planned.
But South Carolina has been fighting about its capitol’s Confederate flag for decades.
According to one analysis — “The South Carolina Confederate Flag: The Politics of Race and Citizenship,” published in the journal Politics and Policy in 2001 — the Confederate flag atop the State House often came up in election years.
