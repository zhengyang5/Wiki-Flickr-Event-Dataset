WASHINGTON, June 18, 2015 /PRNewswire-USNewswire/ -- The Council on American-Islamic Relations (CAIR), the nation's largest Muslim civil rights and advocacy organization, today expressed solidarity with the African-American community following last night's deadly terror attack at a Charleston, S.C., church.
"We stand in solidarity with the African-American community as it and our nation deal with this heartbreaking attack on men and women seeking spiritual growth in a historic house of worship," said CAIR National Board Chair Roula Allouch.
"Our nation's leaders must take immediate concrete measures to deal with the growing divisions within our society that lead to such tragedies," said CAIR National Executive Director Nihad Awad.
He said CAIR is offering to send its publication, "Best Practices for Mosque and Community Safety," to leaders of houses of worship of all faiths.
