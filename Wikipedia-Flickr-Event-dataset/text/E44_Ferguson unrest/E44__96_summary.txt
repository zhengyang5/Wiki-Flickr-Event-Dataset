Please enable Javascript to watch this videoST. LOUIS, MO (KTVI) - Protesters gathered near the Ferguson police department hours before Prosecutor Robert McCulloch announced that Officer Darren Wilson was not indicted.
Protesters made good on their promise to burn the city if the decision did not go their way.
A different tactic was taken in South St. Louis.
They closed all traffic on I-44 at Grand Ave. Demonstrators then marched towards the South Grand business district about an hour later.
Twenty nine people have been arrested so far around Ferguson, St. Louis County Police said early Tuesday morning.
