ST. LOUIS (KMOX) – The St. Louis County Emergency Operations Center plans to activate later today in anticipation of planned weekend demonstrations, which Ferguson, Missouri, protestors are calling a “weekend of resistance.”

Click here to view details of the organized events.

The EOC plans to activate at 4 p.m. and remain activated until early Tuesday morning.

St Louis City, at this point, is not planning to activate it’s Emergency Operations Center, but it will monitor events and make decisions about activation as needed, according to a news release from the State Emergency Management Agency.

The agency also has been coordinating with the Red Cross, preparing for the possibility of opening shelters for residents who feel unsafe in their homes because of civil unrest.
