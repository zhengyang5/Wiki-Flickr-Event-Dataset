The move comes amid high security following the two terrorist bombings in Volgograd widely seen as the work of Islamic extremists.
The Siberian troops are due to start their Olympic security duties next week and are already in place in Sochi.
'It involves primarily protection of and prevention of unauthorised access to sports facilities, residential areas, thoroughfares and bridges,' Siberian Regional Commander Viktor Strigunov told reporters, as cited by Interfax.
The Russian Interior Ministry's Internal Troops is reported to have a 10,000-strong force to provide security at Sochi Olympics.
