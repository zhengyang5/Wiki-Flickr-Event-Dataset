The official death toll from the epic quake and devastating tsunami that hit Japan four days ago reached 1,720 on Monday.
The quake and tsunami disabled the coolant systems at Fukushima Daiichi, which is about 260 km (160 miles) north of Tokyo.
Friday's quake is the strongest in recorded history to hit Japan, according to USGS records that date to 1900.
The world's largest recorded quake took place in Chile on May 22, 1960, with a magnitude of 9.5, the agency said.
