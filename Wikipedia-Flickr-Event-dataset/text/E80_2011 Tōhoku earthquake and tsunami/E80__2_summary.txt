Japan's megaquake: what we knowRead more: "Special report: After Japan's megaquake"0930 GMT, 12 March 2011Sumit Paul-Choudhury, editor, newscientist.comThere has been a massive explosion at the Fukushima-Daiichi nuclear power plant, raising fears of a radioactive meltdown.
As the quake struck, seismic sensors triggered shutdowns at 15 of Japan's nuclear power stations.
After a nuclear plant is shut down, control rods are normally inserted into the reactor core to quench the fission reaction.
Nearer the epicentre, the Onagawa nuclear plant in Miyagi was pictured with smoke streaming from its turbine building.
Five nuclear power plants in northern Japan have been shut down, but there are no reports of leaked chemicals.
