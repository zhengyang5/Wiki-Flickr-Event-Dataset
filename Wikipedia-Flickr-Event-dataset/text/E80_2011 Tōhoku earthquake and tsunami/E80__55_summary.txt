Rescuers struggled to reach survivors on Saturday morning as Japan reeled after an earthquake and a tsunami struck in deadly tandem.
(In Pics: Japan earthquake triggers tsunami) The death toll from the tsunami and earthquake, the strongest ever recorded in Japan, was in the hundreds, but Japanese news media quoted government officials as saying that it could rise to more than 1,300, most of them drowned.
(Watch: Biggest quake in 140 years) On Friday, at 2:46 p.m. Tokyo time, the quake struck.
According to a newspaper, the Mainichi Shimbun, about 600 people were on the roof of a public grade school, in Sendai City.
Fumiaki Yamato, 70, was in his second home in a mountain village outside of Sendai when the earthquake struck.
