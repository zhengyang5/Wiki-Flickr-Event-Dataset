DISCUSSION AND 48-HOUR OUTLOOK ------------------------------ At 1100 PM EDT (0300 UTC), the center of Tropical Storm Joaquin was located near latitude 25.8 North, longitude 71.7 West.
On the forecast track, the center of Joaquin is expected to move near or over portions of the central Bahamas Wednesday night or Thursday.
Additional strengthening is expected during the next 48 hours, and Joaquin is forecast to become a hurricane later tonight or Wednesday.
HAZARDS AFFECTING LAND ---------------------- WIND: Tropical storm conditions could reach portions of the Central Bahamas by Wednesday night, with hurricane conditions possible by late Wednesday night or early Thursday.
RAINFALL: Joaquin is expected to produce 4 to 8 inches of rain with isolated maximum amounts of 12 inches possible over San Salvador and Rum Cay through Thursday morning.
