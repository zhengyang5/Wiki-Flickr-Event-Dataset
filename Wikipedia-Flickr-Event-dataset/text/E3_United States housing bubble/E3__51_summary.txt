Shares of Bear Stearns closed down $2.06, to $143.75; the stock was down more than 4 percent for the week.
For Bear Stearns, the drama surrounding its two troubled hedge funds has given it and its prestigious mortgage business a black eye.
In fact, executives at Bear Stearns Asset Management had debated last summer whether to start the second hedge fund.
So, in August, the Bear Stearns High-Grade Structured Credit Enhanced Leveraged Fund — the second fund that eventually had huge losses — was started with $600 million in investments, mostly from wealthy individual clients of Bear Stearns, and at least $6 billion in money borrowed from banks and brokerage firms.
