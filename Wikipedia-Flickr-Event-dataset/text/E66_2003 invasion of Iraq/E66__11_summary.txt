Interviews I did with officers and soldiers in Baghdad also suggest the military death toll exceeded the civilian.
The imbalance was not as marked as in the first Gulf war when around 3,500 Iraqi civilians were killed, compared with 100,000 soldiers.
Stationed at the al-Taji airbase north of Baghdad, Private Abbas Ali Hussein was a private in an artillery unit.
Like many other Baghdad soldiers, Private Hussein used to go home during the war for food and clean clothes.
"We don't do body counts", said General Tommy Franks, the US commander.
