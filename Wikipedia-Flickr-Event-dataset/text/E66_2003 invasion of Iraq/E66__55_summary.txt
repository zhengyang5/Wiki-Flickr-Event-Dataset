In his prime-time press conference last week, which focused almost solely on Iraq, President Bush mentioned Sept. 11 eight times.
He referred to Saddam Hussein many more times than that, often in the same breath with Sept. 11.
Sources knowledgeable about US intelligence say there is no evidence that Hussein played a role in the Sept. 11 attacks, nor that he has been or is currently aiding Al Qaeda.
In a Knight Ridder poll, 44 percent of Americans reported that either "most" or "some" of the Sept. 11 hijackers were Iraqi citizens.
Future falloutIn the end, will it matter if some Americans have meshed together Sept. 11 and Iraq?
