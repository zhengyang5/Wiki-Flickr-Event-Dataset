Abu Humam al-Shami, leader of al-Qaeda-linked al-Nusra Front in Syria, was reportedly killed in an explosion near Idlib.
File photoARA NewsDamascus, Syria – On Thursday, a huge explosion hit a building where leaders of the al-Nusra Front (Syria’s branch of al-Qaeda) were holding a meeting in the countryside of Idlib, northwest of Syria, pro-Nusra sources reported.
The Front said in a statement online that their military commander in Syria, Abu Humam al-Shami, was killed in the attack.
The Command didn’t mention any new strikes against headquarters of al-Nusra in Idlib.
The international coalition had targeted previously the headquarters of the al-Nusra Front in the countryside of Idlib and Aleppo in northern Syria several times.
