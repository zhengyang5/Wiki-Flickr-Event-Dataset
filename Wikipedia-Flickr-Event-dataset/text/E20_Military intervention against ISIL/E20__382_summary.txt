U.S. officials confirm that two Air Force stealth B-2 bombers struck two ISIS camps in Libya 28 miles south of Sirte on Wednesday night.
Add InterestThe airstrikes targeted 80 to 100 ISIS fighters in multiple camps south of the coastal city of Sirte, which was once an ISIS stronghold, an official told ABC News.
Many of the ISIS fighters in the camps fled Sirte during a successful siege of the city last fall, according to another official.
Defense Secretary Ash Carter told reporters at a Pentagon briefing that more than 80 ISIS fighters were killed in the airstrikes.
U.S. officials have said the number of ISIS fighters in Libya likely totals 1,000.
