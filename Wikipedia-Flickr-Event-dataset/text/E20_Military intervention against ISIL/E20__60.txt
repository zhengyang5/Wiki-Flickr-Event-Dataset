Iraqi state television reported that U.S.-led airstrikes against Islamic State leaders near Mosul left the group’s leader wounded. WSJ's Matt Bradley reports. Photo: European Pressphoto Agency

The U.S.-led coalition targeted Islamic State leaders near the militant stronghold of Mosul in northern Iraq in one of a series of weekend airstrikes, and Iraqi state television reported the group’s leader was wounded in one of the attacks.

U.S. officials said they couldn’t confirm the reports that Abu Bakr Al-Baghdadi, the head of Islamic State, had been hit in an airstrike on Friday night.
