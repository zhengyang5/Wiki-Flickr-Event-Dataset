Tickets go on sale Tuesday for the 2018 Super Bowl Experience, an “interactive football theme park” coming to Minneapolis in February to coincide with the big game, according to a news release issued Monday.

Beginning its week-long run at the Minneapolis Convention Center on Jan. 27, Super Bowl Experience features interactive games, youth football clinics, merchandise from the NFL Shop, player autograph sessions and a chance to take a photo with the Vince Lombardi Trophy, the news release said.

“This ultimate NFL theme park is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for fans to experience the unparalleled energy of Super Bowl Week,” said Peter O’Reilly, the NFL’s senior vice president of events, in the news release. “We have designed new games and attractions that will transport fans of all ages onto the field, under the helmet and closer to all of the fun and excitement surrounding Super Bowl LII.”

Tickets for Super Bowl Experience are available through TicketMaster and cost $35 for adults and $25 for children 12 and under. Tickets permit entry for a specific date and time. Hours of operation vary and will be available at SuperBowl.com.

Super Bowl LII will be played at U.S. Bank Stadium in downtown Minneapolis on Feb. 4.
