The group of scientists, dozens strong, came to Washington DC on 21 January to join the Women’s March, a massive protest against the brand-new US President, Donald Trump.
Trump, who took office on 20 January, has questioned the science underlying climate change and suggested a link between childhood vaccinations and autism.
Members of the group, which is trying to build networks between women scientists in different countries, participated in Women’s March events all over the world on 21 January.
The group says that it had 150–200 scientists at the Washington DC march; another organization, the Association for Women in Science, had at least 30 people marching.
