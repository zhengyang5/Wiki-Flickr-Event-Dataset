New York City Bodegas Strike To Protest Trump's Travel BanEnlarge this image toggle caption Spencer Platt/Getty Images Spencer Platt/Getty ImagesYemeni-owned bodegas across New York City's five boroughs shut their doors at noon ET Thursday to protest President Trump's executive order barring travelers from seven Muslim-majority countries.
For Sulaiman Al-Audi, who works at the Best and Tasty Grocery on 143rd Street, the travel ban sends the message that he is not welcome here.
Audi says he has started the application process to bring his wife and baby to the U.S.
"She was awaiting her visa and now because of the ban she won't be able to join him and her children."
Many bodega workers and their supporters have gathered for a rally at in Brooklyn Borough Hall in solidarity with the Yemeni-American community.
