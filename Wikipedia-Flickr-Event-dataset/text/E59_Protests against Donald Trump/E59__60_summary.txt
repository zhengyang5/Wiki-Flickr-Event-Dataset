Since the Women’s March on Washington on Jan. 21, 2017, we have recorded more than 8,700 protests in the United States through Dec. 31, 2017.
Moreover, there were some surprises among red states with noticeably higher ranks in protest events compared with total state population.
While the media offer more coverage of the occasional violent protest, the overwhelming majority of crowds engaged in nonviolent resistance.
Of course, some nonviolent protests were nevertheless highly disruptive.
That’s why the average nonviolent resistance campaign has so many more active participants than the average armed rebel movement, for instance.
