Seven people are dead after a gunman drove through a college town firing shots.

The shooter was confirmed by family members to be Elliot Rodger, 22, the British-born son of Peter Rodger, assistant director of the Hollywood blockbuster franchise the Hunger Games.

Alan Shifman, lawyer for Peter Rodger, said Elliot had “highly functional Asperger’s syndrome” diagnosed as a child. His mother contacted police and a social worker after recent video posts to YouTube, the lawyer said.

Mr Shifman said police investigated the matter and had interviewed Elliot Rodger, finding he had no history with guns but did have trouble making friends.
