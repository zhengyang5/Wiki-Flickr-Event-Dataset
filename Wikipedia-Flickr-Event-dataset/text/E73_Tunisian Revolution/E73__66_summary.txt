A state of emergency is in place in Tunisia tonight after another day of bloody clashes between security forces and protesters which forced the president to dismiss his entire government.
Amid escalating violence in the capital, Tunis, the state news agency said the president, Zine al-Abidine Ben Ali, would call fresh legislative elections in six months.
He also declared the state of emergency, which bans more than three people from gathering together in the open and imposes a night-time curfew.
