Members of the Islamist Ennahdha movement among those set to go free and could have role in Tunisia's political futureTunisia's new government appears on the brink of releasing political prisoners, including all members of the Islamist Ennahdha movement.
Najib Chebbi, an opposition party leader who has joined the new government, claimed that all prisoners had been released.
A prominent figure in Ennahdha said it had been unable to confirm the release but was hopeful it would happen shortly.
Samir Dilou, a lawyer and Ennahdha leader, said: "We've spoken to the families.
Activists inside and outside of Tunisia, including Dilou, have suggested in recent days that the party might participate in legislative elections, perhaps in partnership with other secular parties, but it would not run in presidential elections.
