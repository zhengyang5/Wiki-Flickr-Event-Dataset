Rioting spreads across Tunisia; unrest also reported in AlgeriaTunisian youths clash with police in several cities as teachers and lawyers go on strike over high unemployment and food prices.
At the root of the unrest in Tunisia is discontent with the autocratic government's management of the economy.
Reports also trickled out about similar unrest in neighboring Algeria, where rioting youths this week burned shops in the capital and clashed with police in several cities.
Protesters also rioted in the town of Makthar, about 90 miles southwest of Tunis, the journalist said.
