In its scale and planning, the violence in Paris — which has left 132 people dead — marks a shift beyond anything seen from the jihadis.
As with much of Isis’s terror, the attack and the horrific imagery it has produced were designed to send a message with dual meanings.
Such attacks, according to Isis’s terror playbook, are aimed at causing political division as much as they are likely to strengthen enemies’ resolve.
The attacks, Isis’s message says to them, invoking Allah and the prophet 16 times in its claim of responsibility, struck at “idolators”.
Isis, it showed, would strike at the elements of Western society that made them most uncomfortable.
