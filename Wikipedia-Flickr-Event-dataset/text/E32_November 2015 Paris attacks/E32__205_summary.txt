The rest of the country was put on a level three alert, one level short of the maximum.
The measures are thought to have been implemented over fears that Salah Abdeslam is hiding in the Brussels area.
“Following a new assessment, the terror alert level has been raised to level four, very serious, for the Brussels region,” said a government statement.
By the afternoon, about half of city-centre shops had closed , while armoured personnel carriers restricted movement in the old city centre.
The mayors of Brussels’ 19 boroughs met at least twice and recommended a thorough lockdown of the city, not all of which was observed.
